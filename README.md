### My Story

Ever since I was a little kid, cybersecurity has been my passion. I still remember sitting in the computer lab of my school in grade nine and learning about the term "ethical hacking". The fact that I can break into computer systems, legally, the cybersecurity bug caught me. As I went through basic tutorials, it was almost magic, being able to get the computer to do whatever I wanted, limited only by my own imagination and ingenuity.

My passion for cybersecurity led me to Python, I started learning to code. I still remember sitting in front of a computer, making it print "Hello World!" for the very first time. Throughout high school and university, I latched on to any ideas for projects I could get, moving from "Hello World!" all the way to a simple web crawler, with many more projects in between. I started learning by tearing through any online resource I could find on anything remotely related to cybersecurity or software development (I don't like the traditional approach to learning). I was insatiable.

This passion carried me through my education at MRIU. Through IBMs' specially crafted course, I studied information security fundamentals, digital forensics, application security, data security, system security, information security auditing, all with pretty much the same fascination that drove me as a kid. And it was here that I learned truly how much there was yet to learn.

So, now, as I look to join the industry, this passion remains with me. There's certainly yet more to learn, yet more to secure, yet more problems to solve, and yet more to build. And for this, I am very grateful.
